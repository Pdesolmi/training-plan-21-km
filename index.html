Race result — April 12, 2026

Official time: 1:43:30 (chip time). Watch showed 1:45:08 including transition area. New PR by 2:11 over the 2025 time of 1:45:41.

The race: Started controlled at 5:01/km (km 1), then ran aggressive through km 4-8 (4:42-4:51/km — the fastest stretch). The hilly 2-loop course had a brutal climb at km 2/9/15-16 (+27-32m elevation). Second loop was harder — km 16 was the slowest at 5:28 on the steepest climb. Hung tough through km 17-21 and sprinted the final 100m.

What worked: 6-week structured training block with 3 quality weeks (W1-W3) before illness hit. Despite losing 10 days to two illnesses in W4-W5, the fitness from W1-W3 was enough. The HR recovery trend (153→143→141) confirmed readiness.

Next time: Holding back km 4-8 (ran 4:42-4:49, plan said 5:00-5:05) could save 30-60s on the second loop climbs and potentially break 1:42.

🍝 Carb Loading — April 9, 10, 11

3 days before race. Increase carbs to 7–8g per kg bodyweight/day. Target ~525–600g carbs/day (if ~75kg).

How

Extra rice, pasta, bread, potatoes at every meal. White carbs over brown (less fiber = less GI risk). Keep protein moderate, reduce fat slightly. Stay hydrated. Nothing new — only foods you know.

Example Day

Breakfast: Oatmeal + banana + honey + toast (~80g)
Snack: Rice cakes + jam (~40g)
Lunch: Pasta with chicken + bread (~120g)
Snack: Banana + energy bar (~50g)
Dinner: Rice + fish + bread (~100g)
Evening: Toast with honey (~30g)

Night Before (April 11)

Pasta or rice dinner (familiar food), extra bread, hydrate well, sleep early.
